Support Driven Compensation Survey

Earlier this year the compenstation survey for the Support Driven Community was passed around. In the survey Support Pros from around the world answered questions regarding their experiance, company size, city size and other factors that go into a salary. Through 61 respones the aveage salary of someone who has completed the survey is $65,874. Today I break down the survey in greater detail.

Male v. Female Pay

To me the question I was most interested in learning was "What sex earns more?" I was increbibly happy to see there wasn't an extreme male pay slant.

The Averages:

  • Female $69,591
  • Male $63,688

The Median:

  • Female $60,000
  • Male $56,700

Company Size

The next interesting stat I pulled was pay compared to company size. I always assumed working at a larger company meant more money and this helped affirm this assumption.

The Averages:

  • Small $61,339
  • Medium $71,369
  • Large+ $74,856

The Median:

  • Small $57,600
  • Medium $60,000
  • Large+ $77,000

* As a note I combined the Very Small & Small categories as well as Large & Big Corporate when getting these numbers.

Experiance

The Support Driven community seems to confirm an assumption of mine - The more you know, the more you'll be paid. In the experiance section those counted themselves as seeing Support as a way of life earned much more than new comers to the field.

The Averages:

  • New $49,125
  • Few Years $60,967
  • Way of Life $76,574

The Median:

  • New $49,250
  • Few Years $55,000
  • Way of Life $70,000

Working Remotely Pay

Looking at another variable I was curious if working remotely had a major change in pay. It doesn't seem as though working remote gives you a big pay bump nor does it hurt you.

The Averages:

  • remote $63,560
  • non remote $67,031

The Median:

  • remote $62,500
  • non remote $57,600
